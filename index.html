About

I am a researcher working on machine learning methods for causal discovery, conditional independence testing, and biological perturbation modeling. My current work studies how learned statistical or generative models can be integrated with structured causal algorithms while keeping evaluation grounded in graph recovery, out-of-distribution prediction, and reproducible experimental settings.

My recent interests include PC-aware neural conditional-independence oracles, federated causal structure learning under heterogeneous observational data, and flow-matching approaches for single-cell perturbation prediction.

I am open to research discussions, collaboration, and careful paper-level feedback. Please feel free to contact me.
